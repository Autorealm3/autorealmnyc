How to Import a JDM Car to the USA: The 2026 Complete Guide

Auto Realm · Updated June 2026 · 12 min read

The dream of owning a right-hand-drive Nissan Skyline GT-R, Toyota Supra, or Mazda RX-7 is more achievable than most people think — but the process is full of pitfalls that can cost you thousands if you get it wrong. Here's how importing a JDM car to the United States actually works in 2026.

The 25-Year Rule

The single most important law for JDM imports is the 25-year exemption. Any vehicle that is at least 25 years old is exempt from federal motor vehicle safety standards (FMVSS) and EPA emissions requirements, making it legal to import. As of 2026, that means vehicles manufactured in 2001 or earlier qualify. This is why the legendary R34 Skyline (1999–2002) is finally becoming widely importable.

Sourcing the Right Car

Most JDM imports come through Japanese auto auctions like USS, TAA, and JAA — the same auctions dealers use. Buying through these requires a licensed exporter or an agent on the ground in Japan who can inspect, bid, and handle export paperwork. Key things to verify:

  • Auction grade — sheets rate cars from 1 (rough) to 5 (excellent). Grade 4 or higher is ideal for a clean example.
  • Mileage authenticity — Japanese auction records are notably honest, but always confirm.
  • Rust history — northern Japan cars can carry winter rust; southern/coastal cars often stay cleaner.

Shipping & Customs

Cars are shipped either via RoRo (roll-on/roll-off) or in a shared/dedicated container. Container shipping costs more but protects the vehicle. Once it lands at a US port, you'll clear customs and pay a 2.5% import duty on the car's value. A customs broker makes this far smoother.

Auto Realm handles the entire process end-to-end — sourcing, auction bidding, inspection, shipping, customs, and final delivery to your door. You tell us the dream car; we make it appear in your driveway.

What It Really Costs

Beyond the purchase price, budget for: auction/agent fees, inland Japan transport, ocean freight ($1,500–$3,500 depending on method), the 2.5% duty, customs brokerage, and US-side registration. A clean grade-4 R34 that hammers for ¥6M at auction can realistically land in the US for a meaningfully higher all-in figure once every cost is counted — so always work from a complete quote, not just the auction price.

Registration & Titling

Once the car clears, you'll title and register it in your state. Some states (like New York) are straightforward for 25-year vehicles; others have extra emissions or inspection steps. We guide every client through their specific state's process.

Choosing your tier comes down to the experience you want:

Renting an Exotic Car in NYC: What First-Timers Need to Know

Auto Realm · Updated June 2026 · 8 min read

Whether it's a Lamborghini Urus for a wedding, a Corvette C8 for a weekend, or an AMG for a photoshoot, renting an exotic in New York is a thrill — but the rules are different from a normal rental. Here's what to expect.

Age & License Requirements

For self-drive exotic and luxury rentals, drivers generally need to be 21 or older with a valid driver's license. Some higher-tier vehicles may have higher age minimums. You'll also provide your license at booking so we can verify before handover.

Insurance Is Non-Negotiable

Self-drive rentals require full-coverage auto insurance (liability, collision, and comprehensive) that meets or exceeds New York requirements. If you don't carry your own full coverage, we'll talk through your options — but the car never leaves without coverage in place.

Deposits & How They Work

Exotic rentals carry a refundable security deposit (typically $1,000, more on higher-value cars) collected at pickup. It's returned after the car comes back clean, on time, undamaged, and with no tolls or violations outstanding. Refunds are processed within the window stated in your agreement.

Pickup vs. Delivery

You choose: pick the car up yourself, or have it delivered to your door, hotel, or venue anywhere in the NYC / NJ / CT area. Delivery is the move for weddings, shoots, and special occasions where you want the car to simply arrive.

Want a chauffeur instead? Many of our luxury and SUV models — S580, G63, Escalade, Range Rover — are available with a professional driver by the hour, so you can enjoy the ride without driving it yourself.

Mileage & Use

Most rentals include a daily mileage allowance (commonly 100–125 miles/day) with a modest per-mile charge beyond it, and are limited to the tri-state area unless arranged otherwise. Read your agreement — knowing the terms keeps your full deposit safe.
