How Much Does It Cost to Rent an Exotic Car in NYC?

Auto Realm · Updated July 2026 · 8 min read

It's the first question everyone asks: what does it actually cost to rent an exotic or luxury car in New York? The honest answer is "it depends" — but not in a vague way. A handful of clear factors set the price, and once you understand them you can budget confidently and avoid the surprises that catch first-timers off guard.

What Sets the Daily Rate

The single biggest factor is the car itself. A luxury flagship, a super-SUV, and a mid-engine sports car all sit at different price points. As a real-world reference from our own fleet, a Mercedes-Benz S580 AMG runs around $495/day, a 2026 Corvette C8 around $500/day, and higher-end exotics like a Lamborghini Urus scale up from there. Rarer and higher-value cars command more — that's true at every exotic rental company.

Duration Changes Everything

Daily pricing is designed for short rentals. If you need a car for a week or a month, always ask about weekly and monthly rates — the effective per-day cost drops significantly the longer you keep the vehicle. A three-day weekend and a full month are priced very differently.

The Costs Beyond the Sticker

The rental rate is only part of the picture. Budget for these too:

  • Security deposit — refundable, typically starting around $1,000 and higher on more valuable cars. You get it back after the car returns clean, on time, and undamaged.
  • Insurance — self-drive rentals require full-coverage auto insurance. If you carry your own, you're set; if not, we'll walk through options.
  • Delivery — pickup is free, but door-to-door delivery to your home, hotel, or venue may carry a fee depending on distance.
  • Mileage — most rentals include a daily allowance (commonly 100–125 miles) with a modest per-mile charge beyond it.

Want a firm, all-in number? Tell us the car, the dates, and whether you want delivery, and we'll send a complete quote with no hidden fees — usually within a few minutes.

How to Get the Best Value

Book on weekdays when demand is lower, keep the car for multiple days to unlock better rates, and consider a chauffeur package if you only need the car for a few hours — for a wedding, shoot, or night out, hourly chauffeur service can cost less than a full-day self-drive rental while giving you a professional driver. And always work from a written quote, not a ballpark, so the number you're told is the number you pay.

How to Import a JDM Car to the USA: The 2026 Complete Guide

Auto Realm · Updated July 2026 · 12 min read

The dream of owning a right-hand-drive Nissan Skyline GT-R, Toyota Supra, or Mazda RX-7 is more achievable than most people think — but the process is full of pitfalls that can cost you thousands if you get it wrong. Here's how importing a JDM car to the United States actually works in 2026.

The 25-Year Rule

The single most important law for JDM imports is the 25-year exemption. Any vehicle that is at least 25 years old is exempt from federal motor vehicle safety standards (FMVSS), making it legal to import without meeting modern US crash and equipment standards. As of 2026, that means vehicles manufactured in 2001 or earlier qualify. This is why the legendary R34 Skyline (1999–2002) is finally becoming widely importable.

Sourcing the Right Car

Most JDM imports come through Japanese auto auctions like USS, TAA, and JAA — the same auctions dealers use. Buying through these requires a licensed exporter or an agent on the ground in Japan who can inspect, bid, and handle export paperwork. Key things to verify:

  • Auction grade — sheets rate cars from 1 (rough) to 5 (excellent). Grade 4 or higher is ideal for a clean example.
  • Mileage authenticity — Japanese auction records are notably honest, but always confirm.
  • Rust history — northern Japan cars can carry winter rust; southern/coastal cars often stay cleaner.

Shipping & Customs

Cars are shipped either via RoRo (roll-on/roll-off) or in a shared/dedicated container. Container shipping costs more but protects the vehicle. Once it lands at a US port, you'll clear customs and pay a 2.5% import duty on the car's value. A customs broker makes this far smoother.

Auto Realm handles the entire process end-to-end — sourcing, auction bidding, inspection, shipping, customs, US titling, and final delivery to your door. You tell us the dream car; we make it appear in your driveway, titled and plated.

What It Really Costs

Beyond the purchase price, budget for: auction/agent fees, inland Japan transport, ocean freight ($1,500–$3,500 depending on method), the 2.5% duty, customs brokerage, and US-side registration. Always work from a complete, all-in quote — not just the auction hammer price — so there are no surprises when the car lands.

Registration & Titling

Once the car clears, you'll title and register it in your state. Some states (like New York) are straightforward for 25-year vehicles; others have extra emissions or inspection steps. We guide every client through their specific state's process from start to plates.

Import and registration rules change and vary by state. Treat this as a general overview and confirm current requirements with a licensed importer or your DMV before committing.

Kei Trucks Explained: America's Favorite Mini JDM Import

Auto Realm · Updated July 2026 · 7 min read

They're tiny, they're charming, and they're everywhere right now. Japanese kei trucks — the mini pickups from Honda, Suzuki, Daihatsu, and Subaru — have become one of the hottest imports in the United States. Here's why, and what you need to know before bringing one home.

What Is a Kei Truck?

"Kei" (from kei-jidōsha, "light vehicle") is a Japanese class of ultra-compact vehicles built to strict size and engine limits. Kei trucks pack a surprisingly useful flatbed, a thrifty 660cc engine, and often four-wheel drive into a footprint smaller than most sedans. Think Honda Acty, Suzuki Carry, Daihatsu Hijet, and Subaru Sambar.

Why Everyone Wants One

  • Utility — they haul, they fit anywhere, and 4WD versions are shockingly capable off-road, on farms, and around large properties.
  • Efficiency — that 660cc engine sips fuel compared to a full-size truck.
  • Character — nothing else on an American road looks or feels like a kei truck. They're pure fun.
  • Value — they're an affordable entry point into JDM ownership.

The 25-Year Rule Applies

Like all JDM imports, kei trucks become federally importable once they're 25 years old. Plenty of clean 1990s and early-2000s examples now qualify, which is a big reason the market has taken off.

Registration Varies by State — Read This First

This is the one area where kei trucks get tricky. Registration and on-road use rules differ significantly from state to state — some states register them without issue, others restrict them to off-road or low-speed use, and rules have been changing in recent years. Before you buy, confirm exactly how your state handles kei trucks so you know what you'll be able to do with it.

Auto Realm sources kei trucks directly from Japan — inspected, exported, shipped, and delivered. Tell us the model and spec you're after and we'll find a clean example and guide you through your state's registration path.

Renting an Exotic Car in NYC: What First-Timers Need to Know

Auto Realm · Updated July 2026 · 8 min read

Whether it's a Lamborghini Urus for a wedding, a Corvette C8 for a weekend, or an AMG for a photoshoot, renting an exotic in New York is a thrill — but the rules are different from a normal rental. Here's what to expect.

Age & License Requirements

For self-drive exotic and luxury rentals, drivers generally need to be 21 or older with a valid driver's license. Some higher-tier vehicles may have higher age minimums. You'll also provide your license at booking so we can verify before handover.

Insurance Is Non-Negotiable

Self-drive rentals require full-coverage auto insurance (liability, collision, and comprehensive) that meets or exceeds New York requirements. If you don't carry your own full coverage, we'll talk through your options — but the car never leaves without coverage in place.

Deposits & How They Work

Exotic rentals carry a refundable security deposit (typically $1,000, more on higher-value cars) collected at pickup. It's returned after the car comes back clean, on time, undamaged, and with no tolls or violations outstanding. Refunds are processed within the window stated in your agreement.

Pickup vs. Delivery

You choose: pick the car up yourself, or have it delivered to your door, hotel, or venue anywhere in the NYC / NJ / CT area. Delivery is the move for weddings, shoots, and special occasions where you want the car to simply arrive.

Want a chauffeur instead? Many of our luxury and SUV models — S580, G63, Escalade, Range Rover — are available with a professional driver by the hour, so you can enjoy the ride without driving it yourself.

Mileage & Use

Most rentals include a daily mileage allowance (commonly 100–125 miles/day) with a modest per-mile charge beyond it, and are limited to the tri-state area unless arranged otherwise. Read your agreement — knowing the terms keeps your full deposit safe.
